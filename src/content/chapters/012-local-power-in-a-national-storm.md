## Opening movement

Begin with the half-empty public meeting where decisions are made by the people who showed up.

## Core argument

National politics trains citizens to feel dramatic and powerless. Local power is less glamorous and more available. The citizen who learns the local map becomes harder to pacify.

## Machinery to name

- City councils, school boards, planning boards, utility boards, public comment, budgets, procurement, zoning, emergency planning.
- Tenant associations, unions, co-ops, mutual aid groups, neighborhood watches, local journalism.
- The difference between symbolic politics and applied leverage.

## Practical discipline

- Learn who decides what.
- Attend meetings before crisis.
- Read agendas and budgets.
- Build coalitions around specific demands.
- Keep records.
- Follow up after the cameras leave.

## Ending movement

Hope becomes serious when it learns where decisions are made.
