## Opening movement

Open at the kitchen table with two fixed facts: the rent is due and the debt minimums do not care.

The reader should feel how breathing room disappears before disaster becomes visible.

## Core argument

Debt and rent are not merely expenses. They are claims on future freedom. When too much income is pledged before it arrives, the citizen becomes easier to frighten, easier to discipline, and easier to exploit.

## Machinery to name

- Rent as tribute when housing scarcity gives owners pricing power.
- Credit as both bridge and trap.
- Fees, penalties, and minimum payments as mechanisms that punish thin margins.
- Refinancing, consolidation, and payment plans as tools that can help or deepen exposure.
- The moral confusion that treats every debtor as reckless and every creditor as prudent.

## Practical discipline

- Rank obligations by consequence: shelter, income, transportation, legal standing, and health first.
- Contact creditors or landlords before default when possible.
- Read leases, loan terms, fee schedules, and renewal notices.
- Avoid solving structural gaps with high-cost credit.
- Document every payment, notice, agreement, and repair request.

## Ending movement

The goal is not debt-free purity as performance. The goal is restored room to maneuver. Breathing room is not luxury. It is civic oxygen.
