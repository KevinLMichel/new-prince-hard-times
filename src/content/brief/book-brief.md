`The New Prince of Hard Times` is a gritty, practical field guide for ordinary people living through inflation, layoffs, war, inequality, technological disruption, political theater, housing pressure, broken trust, and the age of permanent crisis.

It is not a book of despair.

It argues that crisis is not merely something to survive. Crisis is the furnace in which sharper citizens, stronger communities, better institutions, and more honest leaders can be made.

The old bargain has cracked. Wages do not stretch. Credentials do not guarantee security. Governments promise stability and deliver improvisation. Corporations preach purpose and fire workers by spreadsheet. Billionaires speak of humanity while hoarding influence. Media empires profit from fear. Politicians convert pain into slogans.

So the citizen must become harder to deceive, harder to exploit, harder to divide, and harder to defeat.

This book teaches how.
