## Opening movement

Do not begin with battlefield spectacle. Begin with the household bill, the veteran in the waiting room, the refugee child in a new school, the cyberattack notice, the fuel price, or the defense contract headline.

## Core argument

War and peace are often decided by elites, but the invoices travel downward. Citizens must learn to read conflict beyond slogans: who benefits, who pays, what tradeoffs are hidden, and what fear is being cultivated.

## Machinery to name

- Defense contracting and the business of instability.
- Energy shocks and sanctions as household pressure.
- Propaganda and censorship pressures in wartime.
- Veterans, trauma, disability, and abandonment after patriotic language fades.
- Refugees and local receiving communities.
- Budget tradeoffs between permanent conflict and domestic repair.

## Practical discipline

- Demand clarity before emotional consent.
- Separate defense from profiteering.
- Refuse to treat veterans as symbols while ignoring their care.
- Watch how war language changes domestic politics.
- Prepare households for cyber, energy, and supply disruptions without panic.

## Ending movement

A serious citizen does not worship war or pretend danger does not exist. The duty is harder: defend what must be defended without letting conflict become a business model for the powerful.
