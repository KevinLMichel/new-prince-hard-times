## Opening movement

Begin with the moment pain is given a target before it is given an explanation.

The rent rises, the job disappears, the school decays, the border becomes a symbol, the city changes, and suddenly a politician or media figure arrives with a simple enemy.

## Core argument

Blame is not always false. Some people and institutions deserve accountability. But blame becomes dangerous when it replaces diagnosis, strategy, and repair.

Hard times create an audience hungry for certainty. The attention merchants and political theater know how to feed that hunger.

## Machinery to name

- Scapegoating as emotional relief.
- Moral panic as political technology.
- Conspiracy shortcuts that turn complexity into possession by hidden enemies.
- Outrage media that monetizes fear.
- Political actors who prefer useful anger to useful citizens.

## Practical discipline

- Ask who benefits from your anger.
- Ask what evidence would change your mind.
- Separate immediate harm from symbolic enemy-making.
- Distrust anyone who offers contempt as belonging.
- Refuse to let real pain be converted into cruelty.

## Ending movement

The citizen of hard times must be angry enough to notice injustice and disciplined enough not to become a weapon in someone else's hand.
