The first draft may move with literary force. The revision draft must move with receipts.

## Research lanes

- Economic data: BLS, BEA, Census, Federal Reserve, FRED, OECD, IMF, World Bank, Eurostat, and national statistics offices.
- Policy and law: Federal Register, Congressional Research Service, agency guidance, court filings, state and local public records.
- Current events: Reuters, AP, BBC, Financial Times, Wall Street Journal, New York Times, local newspapers, and trade press.
- Corporate and labor: SEC filings, earnings calls, WARN notices, union releases, NLRB records, and company statements.
- Housing and debt: HUD, CFPB, Federal Reserve household debt data, Census housing data, eviction research, and local housing authorities.
- War and supply chains: defense budgets, contract data, shipping indexes, energy data, commodity reports, NATO/EU sources, and credible conflict-monitoring sources.
- Human texture: interviews, oral histories, local reporting, nonprofit reports, mutual-aid groups, and careful anonymized accounts.

## Guardrails

- Use real names only for public figures, public officials, executives, agencies, companies, courts, or documented entities where the source record supports relevance.
- Avoid motive claims unless directly sourced.
- Phrase allegations with attribution: "according to," "the agency reported," "the complaint alleges," "the company announced," or "workers told reporters."
- Require at least two sources for contested or reputation-affecting claims, with one primary source where possible.
- Mark claims involving crime, corruption, fraud, discrimination, abuse, bad faith, or personal misconduct as `high` risk and `needs legal review`.
- Prefer institutional critique over personal accusation when the argument is systemic.

## Chapter source packets

Before a chapter moves from `draft` to `revision`, create or update a source packet if it uses current figures, named institutions, named public figures, war claims, housing claims, labor claims, or inequality claims.

Each packet should include:

- Facts checked through date.
- Claims linked to claim-ledger IDs.
- Sources used and sources rejected.
- Volatile claims to re-check before publication.
- Passages that need human/legal review.
