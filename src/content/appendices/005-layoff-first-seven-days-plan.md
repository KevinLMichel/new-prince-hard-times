Job loss is an event. Do not let it become a verdict.

## Seven-day stabilizer

| Day | Action | Done? | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Day 1 | Read documents. Do not sign what you do not understand. Save contacts, benefits information, severance papers, and final pay details. | &nbsp; | &nbsp; |
| Day 2 | File for unemployment or relevant benefits if eligible. | &nbsp; | &nbsp; |
| Day 3 | List cash, bills, deadlines, health coverage, and household risks. Tell trusted people directly that you are looking. | &nbsp; | &nbsp; |
| Day 4 | Update resume, references, and proof of work. | &nbsp; | &nbsp; |
| Day 5 | Build a simple tracking sheet for applications and calls. | &nbsp; | &nbsp; |
| Day 6 | Apply steadily, not wildly. Contact people who know your work. | &nbsp; | &nbsp; |
| Day 7 | Reset the rhythm: search, apply, contact, move the body, handle one household task, and stop before panic turns the day into fog. | &nbsp; | &nbsp; |
| Week 2 | First follow-up list. | &nbsp; | &nbsp; |

## First rule

Do not disappear from the people who know your work.
