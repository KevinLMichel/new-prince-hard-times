## Opening movement

Begin with the person who leaves work early for a sick child, the adult child managing an elder's medicine, or the disabled relative whose needs do not fit the spreadsheet.

## Core argument

Hard times reveal that the economy rests on unpaid and underpaid care. Children, elders, disabled people, and the sick expose the lie that households are merely consumer units.

## Machinery to name

- Childcare costs and school instability.
- Eldercare, disability care, and caregiver burnout.
- Medical bureaucracy and family logistics.
- The way crisis shifts care burdens into households.
- The emotional economy of guilt, obligation, and exhaustion.

## Practical discipline

- Build care maps: who depends on whom, for what, and when.
- Document medical, school, legal, and benefit information.
- Create backup plans for transportation, medicine, childcare, and emergency contacts.
- Protect caregivers from becoming invisible infrastructure.
- Teach children calm boundaries rather than panic.

## Ending movement

A society is judged not only by what it builds, but by who must quietly break to keep it running.
